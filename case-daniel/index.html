The Situation

A founder buried in his own data

Daniel runs a real estate business. He is not behind on technology. He uses Claude every day. On long drives he talks his ideas into it, and he has built up a pile of notes, data, and half-started opportunities. He is the opposite of stuck for ideas.

What he didn't have was a way to move on the hunches.

The Problem

Hunches he couldn't chase

He'd have a feeling that something was off, or that money was sitting somewhere it shouldn't be, and then hit the same wall every time. To chase it, he'd have to pull people off their work, ask pointed questions, and signal to his team that he was digging into something. Some hunches aren't worth raising a flag over until you know they're real. So they sat. Smart guy, good instincts, real data, and no quiet way to look.

The Unlock

I showed him a Little League draft

I didn't pitch him. I showed him something I'd done for my son's baseball team.

I coach, and we run a draft before the season. Right before ours, I got handed five different spreadsheets and two Word documents, all with player data in different shapes. I needed one view. Every player, rank-ordered, so I could run the draft without flipping between files. I was never going to merge all that in Excel by hand. I don't have the time, and it's not a good use of it.

So I dumped all seven files into Claude and told it exactly what I wanted. Three rounds of back-and-forth. One clean board. Maybe thirty minutes start to finish.

That's the whole demo. Not a finance tool. Not an enterprise rollout. A busy dad who didn't want to do data entry for a kids' baseball draft.

What He Did

He went and looked himself

Daniel saw it and something clicked. He had a hunch about inefficiencies in his accounts payable that he'd been sitting on, exactly because checking it the old way meant asking around and tipping his hand. Now he had a way to look himself, quietly, without involving anyone.

He went and did it. Three weeks later we met again and he told me he'd found roughly $900,000 in savings sitting in his own accounts payable.

Why It Worked

The lever, not the labor

I didn't find that money. I never touched his books. Daniel found it.

He had the data, the hunch, and the judgment the whole time. What he was missing wasn't effort or intelligence. It was a lever.
